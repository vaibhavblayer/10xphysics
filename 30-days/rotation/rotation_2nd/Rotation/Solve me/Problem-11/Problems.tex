\begin{question}
A  block of mass $m$ moves with a velocity $v_0$ on a smooth horizontal surface. It then passes over a cylinder of radius $R$ and mass $m$, capable of rotating about its own fixed axis through $O$. The block, while passing over, slips on the cylinder. The slipping stops before it loses contact with the cylinder. The block then moves on a similar smooth horizontal surface with a velocity $v$. Find the velocity $v$
\end{question}

{\physics}

\draw (2.5,0)--++(1.5,0)--++(0,0.5) edge[->] node[at end,above]{$v$} ++(1,0) --++(0,0.5)--++(-1.5,0)--cycle ;

\begin{center}
{\solution}
\end{center}

\begin{calculations}
Here net external torque on the system about the axis of cylinder is zero, therefore we can apply the conservation of angular momentum.

\begin{align*}
L_i &= L_f \\[3 mm]
mv_0R &= mvR + I\omega \\[3 mm]
mv_0R &= mvR + \dfrac{mR^2}{2} \left( \dfrac{v}{R} \right) \\[3 mm]
			& \textit{when slipping ceases , $v=R\omega$} \\[3 mm]
mv_0 &= \dfrac{3mvR}{2} \\[3 mm]
	v   &= \dfrac{2v_0}{3}
\end{align*}

\end{calculations}
