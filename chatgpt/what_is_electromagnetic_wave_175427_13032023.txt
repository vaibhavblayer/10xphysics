Q. What is electromagnetic wave ?

An electromagnetic wave is a type of wave that consists of oscillating electric and magnetic fields. These waves can travel through a vacuum, such as outer space, or through an object, such as air or water. Electromagnetic waves are a form of energy and can be classified according to their frequency. Examples of electromagnetic waves include radio waves, microwaves, infrared radiation, visible light, ultraviolet radiation, X-rays, and gamma rays.
